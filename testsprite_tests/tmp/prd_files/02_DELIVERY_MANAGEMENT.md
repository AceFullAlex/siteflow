# Delivery Management Guide

As a Logistics Assistant Manager, efficiently handling the incoming deliveries is core to the construction project. Here is a step-by-step checklist to optimize the management of your deliveries.

## 1. Pre-arrival Coordination
- **Review Expected Deliveries:** Start the day by checking the daily delivery tracker against the scheduled arrivals. Ensure you know exactly what is coming (concrete elements, drainage pipes, steel, etc.).
- **Verify Transport Providers:** International drops usually involve overarching transport companies. Acknowledge the estimated time of arrival (ETA) and ensure the site access points are clear for their specific vehicle type.

## 2. Arrival & Document Verification 
- **Check the Associated Documents:** You must receive a CMR (International Consignment Note) for cross-border deliveries or a TAD (Transit Accompanying Document) depending on origin. 
- **Flag Missing Documents Immediately:** It is a frequent occurrence that drivers arrive without necessary cross-border forms. Document the missing paperwork on the delivery note and request a digital copy from the supplier immediately.
- **Count & Compare:** Always compare the physical quantities received against the ordered amounts on the `DELIVERY SHEET`. Record the delivered amounts accurately.

## 3. Discrepancy & Damage Handling
- **Visually Inspect:** Ensure the heavy pipes or precast elements have suffered no transport damage.
- **Endorse the POD:** If items are damaged or missing, clearly annotate the Proof of Delivery (POD) before signing. Take photos of the damage while it is still on the truck. NEVER sign a clean POD if there are issues.

## 4. Post-Delivery Administration
- **Scan and Save:** Digitize the signed paperwork immediately. Create a standard naming convention (e.g., `YYYY-MM-DD_Supplier_Commodity_DeliveryNote_CMR.pdf`).
- **Update the Delivery Sheet:** Input the newly received quantities into the tracking sheet to maintain visibility on cumulative deliveries against the master order.
