# Project Understanding & Scope

## Logistics Management Context
The primary focus is managing the flow of heavy structural materials (e.g., concrete elements, rebar) and below-ground services (e.g., drainage systems, utilities) for large-scale construction projects such as Datacenters. The role involves ensuring a smooth, compliant, and well-documented supply chain from manufacturer to the construction site.

## Deliveries Overview
The materials arriving on site are critical for the foundational and structural phases of the project. Key takeaways for document tracking and verification:
1. **International Deliveries (CMRs):** Deliveries arriving from international borders via transport companies require a CMR (international consignment note). These are crucial legal documents that must be tracked alongside the standard Delivery Note.
2. **Material Types:** Handling heavy materials, precast/RC elements, drainage pipes, and other specialized construction goods.
3. **Tracking Mechanisms:** The main tracking tool is a master `DELIVERY SHEET` which compares **ORDERED vs DELIVERED** quantities across various material IDs over time. 
4. **Safety & RAMS:** Operations are governed by strict safety documents and Method Statements (like RAMS for Loading & Unloading), highlighting that safety compliance during the unloading phase is paramount.

## Key Challenges Identified
- **Missing Paperwork:** Drivers frequently arrive without proper international transport paperwork (missing CMRs) or disorganized delivery packets, which can cause compliance problems.
- **Data Entry & Tracking:** Keeping the delivery tracker constantly updated with incoming deliveries requires high attention to detail to catch discrepancies between ordered amounts and received amounts immediately.
- **Document Scrambling:** Inevitably, large volumes of paperwork can become scrambled, requiring an intelligent system to match separated delivery notes to their corresponding CMRs or TADs securely.
