# Safe Unloading of Trucks

Handling heavy groundworks materials, like large structural elements or deep drainage pipes, presents significant risks during unloading. As Logistics Assistant Manager, your role involves enforcing the **Safe Loading & Unloading RAMS**.

## Pre-Unloading Checks
- **Designate the Unloading Zone:** Ensure the area is level, solid, and free of unnecessary personnel. The site must have clearly demarcated paths for flatbeds and articulated lorries coming from different origins.
- **Ensure the Plant is Ready:** Based on the load, ensure the right lifting equipment (telehandler, forklift, crane) is available and the operator has checked their Lifting Operations Method Statement.
- **Visual Inspection of Load:** Before untying any straps, inspect the load. Some international shipments might shift significantly during long transit. If the load is leaning, do NOT release the straps.

## Establishing The Exclusion Zone
- **Physical Demarcation:** Barricade the unloading area. This is a high-risk zone and pedestrians are strictly forbidden without authorization.
- **Driver Management:** The driver MUST exit the cab during unloading but they must remain in a designated safe spot, preferably in full view of the plant operator and banksman.
- **Banksman Involvement:** All heavy plant movements must be directed by a qualified banksman to ensure blind spots are covered.

## Lifting the Equipment
- **Rigging & Slings:** For heavy components like concrete pipes or large RC parts, only trained riggers/slingers should attach the lifting accessories.
- **Pipes Risk Mitigation:** Because pipes can roll, ensure that side protection on the truck is incrementally lowered. Use chocks and wedges immediately upon placing them on the ground.
- **Clear Communication:** Establish hand signals or radio comms between the slinger and the plant operator (e.g. crane/telehandler driver).
